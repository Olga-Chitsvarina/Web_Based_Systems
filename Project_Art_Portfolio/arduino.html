"POLITE DRIVER"

ABSTRACT: Drivers often feel thankful when other drivers yield to them. They may show their appreciation by lifting a hand or blinking with taillights. I was thinking about different ways to say ‘Thank You’ while driving. I am proposing an innovative and unique project ‘Polite Driver’ (I have never seen anything like that). This project needs a minimum set up: Arduino kit, a button and an LCD screen. The button can be placed on a steering wheel or the instrument panel so that drivers can have easy access to it. A small screen is attached to the backside of the car. When a driver wants to say ‘Thank You’ to another driver, he/she pushes the button, and the screen gets updated with a text ‘Thank You. Have a Good Day’. This simple solution encourages drivers to be more polite and thankful and care about other people on the road.

WHAT DID INSPIRE ME TO CREATE THIS PROJECT?

PUBLIC TRANSPORT: I have often seen LED displays on busses, saying : "Sorry", "Have a Nice Day", "Merry Christmas", "Happy Stampeding", and etc. I also thought that regular cars may also have a LED display and communicate some useful information.
MARK ROBER'S VIDEO: Mark Rober's NICEST Car Horn Ever- DIY:(2017). This video encouraged me to think about being polite and respectful on the roads.
